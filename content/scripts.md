## Script 1 — The Launch PSA
### *"An Important Message from Boston Pasta Lab"*

**WHAT THIS VIDEO DOES**
This is your first post ever. It establishes who Boston Pasta Lab is, what the problem is (drama), and what the solution is (pasta). No hat shown until the very end. Completely straight. No jokes signaled. If someone watches this not knowing it's a bit, they should be genuinely unsure for the first 15 seconds.

---

**EQUIPMENT**
- iPhone (vertical, 9:16)
- Tripod or phone propped against something stable
- One window for natural light OR a basic ring light — positioned in front of the speaker, not behind
- Lapel mic or AirPods mic if available (improves audio quality significantly, not required)

---

**SETTING**
A clean, neutral wall. Off-white or light gray. Nothing on the wall behind them — no art, no shelves, nothing interesting. Institutional. Empty. The absence of personality is the personality. Could be a home office wall, a plain interior wall, or a garage wall that's clean. Film during daylight if using natural light. The frame should feel slightly too close — medium close-up, not a wide shot. Speaker's head and shoulders, centered.

---

**WARDROBE**
Option A: White lab coat over a plain button-down. Most committed to the bit.
Option B: Plain dark button-down, no logos, no patterns. Clean and serious.
No hats during the video until the final end card. No jewelry, nothing distracting. The less interesting the outfit, the better.

**Hair/face:** Normal. Not trying. This is not a beauty video. Brushed is fine. No product.

---

**FACIAL EXPRESSION & BODY LANGUAGE**
Eyes directly into the camera lens the entire time. Not aggressive — calm, measured, like a doctor delivering news. Hands can be at sides or one hand can hold a single sheet of paper (doesn't need to have anything on it). No movement. No swaying. No smiling. The closest emotion is mild concern — like someone who has seen the data and is genuinely troubled by drama levels.

---

**FULL SCRIPT — word for word**

*Deliver each line slowly. Full stop between sentences. Let the silence sit. The pauses are doing half the work.*

> *(camera starts, person is already looking at lens)*
>
> "In 2025, drama reached unprecedented levels."
>
> *(two-second pause)*
>
> "Studies show the average American now encounters seventeen dramatic situations per day."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "The group chat."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "The passive-aggressive email."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "The parking spot."
>
> *(longer pause — three seconds)*
>
> "At Boston Pasta Lab, we've spent years studying this crisis."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "Our findings are clear."
>
> *(two-second pause — look slightly down, then back up to camera)*
>
> "More pasta."
>
> *(one-second pause)*
>
> "Less drama."
>
> *(three-second pause — do not break)*
>
> "We're not just selling a hat."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "We're solving a problem."
>
> *(hold eye contact for two full seconds. Cut.)*

---

**ON-SCREEN TEXT — appears as captions, white text, simple font, bottom third of frame**

| Timestamp | Text on screen |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:03 | `BOSTON PASTA LAB — Cambridge, MA` |
| 0:06 | `17 dramatic situations / day` *(appears under "seventeen dramatic situations")* |
| 0:08 | `The group chat.` |
| 0:10 | `The passive-aggressive email.` |
| 0:12 | `The parking spot.` |
| 0:20 | `FINDING:` |
| 0:22 | `More Pasta. Less Drama.` *(slightly larger, centered)* |
| Final frame | Hat graphic or photo of the hat. Text: `Boston Pasta Lab` below it. `More Pasta, Less Drama.` |

---

**AUDIO**
No music during the video. Silence makes it more serious. Optional: a single low tone or ambient hum that fades in during the final pause — like a documentary score. If you use music, it should be something that sounds like a pharmaceutical ad or a nature documentary — not Italian music, not something fun.

**Final frame:** a brief, clean musical sting. One note. Fade out.

**TOTAL LENGTH:** 28–35 seconds

## Script 2 — The First Lab Report
### *"Boston Pasta Lab — Weekly Research Update, Week 1"*

**WHAT THIS VIDEO DOES**
This establishes your recurring weekly format. Every week, a new Lab Report. The format never changes — only the findings do. This is your content engine. Once this format is locked, you can produce one of these every week with 20 minutes of filming.

---

**EQUIPMENT**
- iPhone (vertical, 9:16)
- Tripod
- Clipboard with paper on it (doesn't need real content — camera won't show it clearly)
- Optional: reading glasses (adds 40% more scientist energy)
- Same lighting setup as Script 1

---

**SETTING**
Two options:

**Option A — The Desk:** Person sitting at a plain desk. Nothing on the desk except the clipboard and possibly a pen. Clean wall behind. Same vibe as Script 1 but seated. Camera at eye level — do not shoot from above or below.

**Option B — Standing, slight angle:** Person standing, facing slightly off-center (30-degree angle to camera), clipboard in hand, occasionally glancing up from clipboard to camera. Feels more like a news briefing.

Pick one and use it for every Lab Report — the format being visually identical every week is part of the bit.

---

**WARDROBE**
Same as Script 1. If you used the lab coat in Script 1, use it here. Establish a uniform for the Lab Report format specifically — viewers should recognize what format they're watching within the first frame.

---

**FACIAL EXPRESSION & BODY LANGUAGE**
Same measured calm as Script 1 — but here you have the clipboard prop to work with. Look at the clipboard for the data points. Look up at camera for the conclusions. This rhythm — down to read, up to deliver — makes it feel like you're actually reporting findings rather than performing.

Do not rush. Each data point gets its own breath.

---

**FULL SCRIPT — word for word**

> *(camera starts — person looking at clipboard, then looks up)*
>
> "Boston Pasta Lab. Weekly Research Update. Week one."
>
> *(look back down at clipboard briefly, then up)*
>
> "Our team introduced a test subject to one bowl of rigatoni. Sunday sauce. Four-hour cook time minimum."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "Pre-pasta drama level: eight out of ten."
>
> *(slight pause — deliver this like it's a concerning number)*
>
> "Subject described feeling —"
>
> *(look at clipboard)*
>
> "— quote: 'a lot going on.'"
>
> *(look up)*
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "Post-pasta drama level: one out of ten."
>
> *(pause — let this land)*
>
> "Subject reported feeling —"
>
> *(look at clipboard)*
>
> "— quote: 'fine, actually.'"
>
> *(look up. Slight pause. This is the moment.)*
>
> "We're calling this a breakthrough."
>
> *(two-second pause)*
>
> "Research continues."
>
> *(pause)*
>
> "More pasta. Less drama."
>
> *(hold. Cut.)*

---

**ON-SCREEN TEXT**

| Timestamp | Text on screen |
|---|---|
| 0:00 | `BOSTON PASTA LAB` *(top of frame, small, like a TV chyron)* |
| 0:01 | `WEEKLY RESEARCH UPDATE — WEEK 1` *(below the Boston Pasta Lab text, smaller)* |
| 0:07 | `TEST SUBJECT: 1` |
| 0:08 | `PASTA: Rigatoni, Sunday Sauce (4hr minimum)` |
| 0:12 | `Pre-pasta drama: 8/10 ⚠️` |
| 0:18 | `Post-pasta drama: 1/10 ✓` |
| 0:24 | `STATUS: BREAKTHROUGH` |
| Final frame | `Lab Release #001 — Coming Soon` |

---

**AUDIO**
Same no-music approach. Optional: a very faint, low institutional tone underneath — something that sounds like elevator music from a government building. Subtle enough that most viewers won't consciously notice it.

**Total length:** 20–25 seconds

---

**TIKTOK CAPTION — copy/paste ready**

**HOW TO REPEAT THIS FORMAT WEEKLY**

Every week, swap out:
- The pasta type
- The dramatic situation the subject was experiencing
- The pre and post drama numbers

The structure, the wardrobe, the setting, the delivery — never change. The repetition is what makes it a show.

## Script 3 — The Intervention
### *"A Boston Pasta Lab Field Intervention"*

**WHAT THIS VIDEO DOES**
This is your most shareable format. Short, visual, universally relatable. Someone is in drama. Pasta fixes it. The voiceover is calm, clinical, observational — like a nature documentary narrating a wildlife moment. This is the one people will send to friends with no context.

---

**EQUIPMENT**
- iPhone (vertical, 9:16)
- Tripod for static shots
- Handheld for the "subject in drama" shot — slight movement makes it feel like documentary footage
- A bowl of pasta that looks genuinely good — this matters. Buy or make real pasta. Good sauce. Steam if possible. This is the hero of the video.
- The hat — worn by the subject at the end OR placed next to them

---

**PEOPLE NEEDED**
- One person playing the "subject" (can be you or anyone)
- Optional: one additional person as the off-screen hand that delivers the pasta

---

**SETTING**

**Shot 1 — The Drama:** A kitchen, living room, or office. Somewhere real. The subject is pacing or sitting with their phone. The background should be slightly messy or lived-in — not styled. Real life is dramatic. The contrast with the clean pasta shot matters.

**Shot 2 — The Pasta Arrives:** Same location or cut to a small table or counter. Clean surface. The bowl of pasta is placed into frame by a hand from off-screen. The hand should be unhurried. No drama in the delivery. Just pasta. Shot from slightly above — you want to see the pasta clearly.

**Shot 3 — Eating:** Subject seated, eating. Medium close-up — face and bowl visible. They are visibly calming down. Not smiling yet — just... settling. The tension leaving their shoulders.

**Shot 4 — Peace:** Same person. Phone is face-down on the table. Eyes closed or looking somewhere calm. The food is mostly eaten. They look like someone who has handled it.

**Shot 5 — The End Card:** The hat, placed next to the empty bowl. Or the person wearing the hat. Still. Clean.

---

**WARDROBE**
Subject: regular clothes. Whatever they'd actually be wearing when drama finds them. Nothing styled. T-shirt, hoodie, whatever is real.

---

**FACIAL EXPRESSIONS — subject, shot by shot**

| Shot | Expression |
|---|---|
| Shot 1 (drama) | Tense. Jaw tight. Eyes on phone. Possibly running hand through hair. Not theatrical — real stressed. |
| Shot 2 (pasta arrives) | Looks at the bowl. A pause. Something shifts. Not a big reaction — just a recognition. |
| Shot 3 (eating) | Focused on the pasta. The eating itself is calming. Shoulders drop. |
| Shot 4 (peace) | Relaxed. Unbothered. Phone face-down. The drama is gone. Not happy — just done with it. |

---

**VOICEOVER**
The voiceover is recorded separately and laid over the footage in editing. It is calm, measured, and slightly slow — David Attenborough narrating a wildlife moment. The voice should be completely unbothered by what it's describing. If you're doing it yourself, record it in a quiet room, close to the mic, after a deep breath.

---

**FULL SCRIPT — word for word, with shot timing**

**[Shot 1 — Subject pacing, on phone, stressed]**
*(hold for 3 seconds before VO begins)*

> VO: "Subject is experiencing drama."

*(pause 1 second)*

> VO: "Classic presentation."

---

**[Cut — Shot 2 — Hand places bowl of pasta on surface in front of subject]**
*(shot holds for 2 seconds — let the pasta be seen)*

> VO: "Our researchers deployed the standard protocol."

*(pause)*

---

**[Cut — Shot 3 — Subject eating, visibly settling]**
*(hold for 3–4 seconds)*

> VO: "Drama levels: falling."

*(pause — let the eating be seen)*

> VO: "Subject is responding well."

---

**[Cut — Shot 4 — Subject relaxed, phone face-down]**
*(hold for 3 seconds)*

> VO: "Research notes: effective."

*(pause — two full seconds)*

> VO: "This has been a Boston Pasta Lab intervention."

*(pause)*

> VO: "More pasta."

*(one-second pause)*

> VO: "Less drama."

---

**[Cut — Shot 5 — Hat next to empty bowl, or subject wearing hat]**
*(hold for 3 seconds. No VO. Let it sit.)*

---

**ON-SCREEN TEXT**

| Timestamp | Text on screen |
|---|---|
| 0:00 | `Boston Pasta Lab — Field Intervention #001` *(small, top of frame)* |
| 0:03 | `Subject: experiencing drama` |
| 0:09 | `Protocol deployed: rigatoni, Sunday sauce` |
| 0:14 | `Drama levels: ↓↓↓` |
| 0:21 | `Status: RESOLVED` |
| 0:24 | `This has been a Boston Pasta Lab intervention.` |
| Final frame | `More Pasta. Less Drama.` centered, clean |

---

**AUDIO**
The voiceover IS the audio — no music underneath during the video. Optional: a single nature-documentary-style orchestral note on the final frame.

**EDITING NOTES**
- Cuts should be clean and unhurried. No fast edits. Let each shot breathe.
- No filters. No color grading. Natural color makes it feel more documentary.
- Subtitles matching the VO should appear as the words are spoken — simple white text, bottom third.
- Total length: 25–30 seconds maximum.

Field Intervention #001 — case closed.
