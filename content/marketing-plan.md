# Boston Pasta Lab — Marketing Plan

---

## Brand Platform

**Community Brand:** Boston Pasta Lab
**Product:** More Pasta, Less Drama hat
**Tagline:** More Pasta, Less Drama
**Mission:** Solving the global drama crisis through pasta
**Brand premise:** Boston Pasta Lab is a legitimate scientific research institution dedicated to eliminating drama through the evidence-based application of pasta. Played completely straight. Always.

**Voice:** Deadpan. Official. Slightly pompous. Never winks at the camera. The joke only works if you commit.

**Aesthetic:** Clean, institutional, with a warm Italian-American undercurrent. Not cold — serious on the surface, but the subject matter is pasta. That tension is the whole thing.

**Approach:** Liquid Death model — commit fully to one absurd premise, name an enemy (drama), make the product (the hat) the proof of belonging.

---

## Audience

**Primary:** 18–35. Italian-American culture adjacent OR pasta-curious. Humor-native. Tired of drama in the cultural sense — the group chat, the algorithm, the overcomplication of everything.

**Secondary:** Boston locals (early credibility), streetwear/lifestyle buyers who respond to drop culture, food content followers who don't want another recipe channel.

**The psychographic that matters:** They bought Liquid Death not because they love water but because they wanted to be the kind of person who drinks Liquid Death. Your buyer puts on the hat for the same reason.

---

## Content Strategy

### The Three Pillars

**1. Lab Reports** *(signature, weekly)*
Short scientific announcements about pasta's effects on drama. One per week minimum. Format is locked — clipboard, straight face, finding, data point, sign-off. Only the findings change week to week.

Examples:
- *"Week 4: Subject introduced to bucatini all'amatriciana. Reported drama-free for 72 hours. Longest recorded stretch in the study."*
- *"Breaking: Researchers confirm overcooked pasta correlates with increased drama. Al dente is not a preference. It is a medical recommendation."*
- *"Lab Update: Test subject attempted drama while eating pasta. Could not sustain it. Research continues."*

**2. Interventions** *(recurring skit, 2x/month)*
Someone is in drama. Pasta arrives. Drama ends. 20-25 seconds. The scenario changes every time — the format never does. Works with any relatable stressor: group chat drama, work email, traffic, bad date.

**3. Field Data** *(community UGC, ongoing)*
Followers are researchers. When they post themselves in the hat eating pasta, they're submitting field data. You repost with official Lab framing:

*"Field data submitted by researcher @[handle]. Location: [city]. Pasta: rigatoni. Drama level reported: zero. We thank them for their service."*

This costs nothing to produce and grows the community simultaneously.

### Platform Breakdown

**TikTok — Primary**
- 3–4 posts/week
- Lab Reports and Interventions live here first
- Respond to comments in character as the Lab
- Adapt the voice to trending sounds/formats — never abandon the voice for a trend

**Instagram — Secondary**
- 3 Reels/week (repurpose TikTok content)
- 1 Story/day — could be a "daily drama reading" (check in on drama levels, recommend pasta accordingly)
- UGC reposting as field data
- Hat drop announcements as static posts with official Lab language

**YouTube Shorts — Passive**
- Auto-publish TikTok content. Zero extra work. Compounds over time.

## Hat Drop Strategy

**Naming convention:** Lab Release #001, Lab Release #002, etc. Each drop is a scientific event.

**Drop size:** Start small. 100–150 hats for Lab Release #001. Scarcity is the feature, not the bug.

**Announcement language:**
> *"Boston Pasta Lab is pleased to announce the release of Lab Release #001. 150 units. Available to verified researchers only. Link in bio. More Pasta, Less Drama."*

No "shop now" energy. Official announcement energy.

**Access structure:**
- Followers get first access via link in bio
- Build an email/waitlist by teasing the drop in the weeks before
- Sell out fast. Make the sellout part of the content.
- *"Lab Release #001: Sold out in 4 hours. Research continues. Lab Release #002 in development."*

**Pricing:** Premium. $45–$65 range positions it as a lifestyle product, not merch. The Liquid Death can costs more than a regular water. Price signals belonging.

---

## Community Framework

**Followers = Researchers**
Never call them fans or followers in your content. They're researchers. They're part of the Lab.

**Membership language:**
- "Join the Lab" not "Follow us"
- "Submit your field data" not "Tag us"
- "Lab Release access" not "shop"

**Community milestone content:**
Use follower milestones as Lab announcements:
> *"Boston Pasta Lab has reached 1,000 researchers. Drama levels in the study group are declining. We are encouraged by these results."*

## Partnerships & Collabs

**The Liquid Death model:** Martha Stewart was their perfect collab — completely unexpected, made people share it purely because it was absurd.

**Your version — find someone unexpected who loves pasta:**
- A Boston sports figure known for being low-drama
- A Boston nonna — the most credible pasta scientist imaginable. Her in a lab coat. Fully committed.
- A local chef who plays it straight — not a review, a "consultation with a leading researcher"
- Eventually: a nationally known figure who is famous for being anti-drama

**Collab format:** Always framed as scientific consultation or guest researcher. Never an ad. Never a review.

## 90-Day Launch Plan

### Month 1 — Establish the Voice
**Goal:** Lock the content format. Build enough of a library that the voice is undeniable. Zero hat mentions. The Lab exists before the product.

- Week 1: Post the Launch PSA
- Week 2: First Lab Report + first Intervention
- Weeks 3–4: 2–3 Lab Reports + 1 Intervention + start engaging in comments in-character
- End of month: 15–20 pieces of content live. Voice is clear.

**Milestone:** 200–500 followers across platforms.

---

### Month 2 — Build the Community
**Goal:** Convert followers into researchers. Tease the first drop.

- Continue Lab Reports weekly
- Start reposting any UGC as field data
- Introduce the waitlist: *"Lab Release #001 is in development. Researchers will be notified first."*
- Begin responding to every comment in Lab voice — this is your growth engine at small scale
- One collab attempt: reach out to someone local, low-stakes, just to get the collab format established

**Milestone:** 1,000–2,500 followers. Email list of 100+.

---

### Month 3 — First Drop
**Goal:** Lab Release #001. Sell out. Make the sellout content.

- Week 1: Official announcement — *"Lab Release #001. 150 units."*
- Week 2: Drop goes live. Link in bio. No aggressive push — one post, one story.
- Week 3: Sell out announcement + first field data from people wearing the hat
- Week 4: Tease Lab Release #002. Research continues.

**Milestone:** Hat sells out. 3,000–5,000 followers. First organic UGC without prompting.

| Metric | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total followers | 500 | 3,000 | 12,000 |
| Engagement rate | 6%+ | 8%+ | 5%+ |
| Email/waitlist | — | 200 | 800 |

**The one metric that matters most in month 1:** Share rate on the Launch PSA. If people are sharing it unprompted, the voice is working. If they're not, something in the delivery is off — adjust before scaling.

---

## What Not to Do

- **Don't break character.** Ever. One "lol jk" kills the whole premise.
- **Don't sell the hat before the community exists.** Content first, product second.
- **Don't chase trends that don't fit the voice.** Adapt the voice to trends, never abandon it for them.
- **Don't post high-production content early.** Lo-fi with a locked voice beats polished with a vague one every time.
- **Don't expand the product line until the hat is established.** One product. Nail it.
- **Don't break character in comments.** If someone asks "is this real?" — respond as the Lab. Always.
